Ribs

About the Recipe

Try a new twist on your go-to rib recipe with our Apple Butter BBQ Ribs, a classic pork and apple flavor combination. Cooked slow on a charcoal grill over indirect heat, tangy dry rub flavors mingle with the sweetness of the homemade apple butter bbq sauce. You should know that the tantalizing aroma of these succulent ribs cna attract the envious attention of your neighbors. Don't say we didn't warn you.

Ingredients

  • 2 Full slabs of baby back ribs
  • 1 Tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 Tablespoon paprika
  • 1/2 Tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 Tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 Teaspoon cayenne powder
  • 1 Teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 Teaspoon dried thyme
  • 3-4 Apple wood chunks

Steps

  1. Preheat your grill to 300Â°F with direct and indirect heat zones . Place an aluminum pan under the grill grate on one side and fill halfway with water. Wearing protective gloves , pour a chimney of hot coals on the other side. Toss a few apple wood chunks on top of the coals.
  2. Remove the membrane under each slab of ribs by inserting a butter knife between the membrane and one of the bones. Grab the membrane with a paper towel and pull to remove it completely.
  3. Combine the brown sugar, salt, pepper, paprika, cayenne powder, ginger and thyme in a small bowl. Apply the rub on both sides of the ribs. Press with your hands for the rub to penetrate the meat.
  4. Place the ribs on the grill grate over indirect heat. Close the lid and cook for 2 hours.
  5. Combine apple butter, ketchup, brown sugar, vinegar, mustard, Worcestershire sauce, salt and pepper in a saucepan. Simmer for 10 minutes and set aside.
  6. After 2 hours of cooking, remove ribs from grill. Place each on a separate sheet of aluminum foil. Pour 1 cup of Apple Butter BBQ Sauce on top of each rack and wrap in foil. Place on indirect heat side of the grill and cook for another hour.
  7. Once your ribs are fully cooked, remove the foil and sear over direct heat for 30 seconds per side to caramelize the BBQ sauce.
  8. Slice between the bones and serve with some grilled potato salad and smoked corn on the cob .
